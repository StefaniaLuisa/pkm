# LinkedIn Ads


| Decrease LinkedIn ad CPC by switching from click to impression objectives |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|

| •	The company: B2B LinkedIn ads agency Tuned Social, working on behalf of a tech company.<br/>	•	The goal: Decrease the CPC of LinkedIn ads that have a website visit objective.<br/>	•	The tactic: Switch ads with a CTR of more than 1% to impression-based bidding.<br/>	•	The result: 55% decrease in CPC, and 30% increase in conversions. |
|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

| What's the fast marketing tactic? |
|-----------------------------------|

| The tech company was running CPC (cost per click) campaigns on LinkedIn, with a goal to drive website visits. These campaigns had a CTR (click-through rate) that ranged between 0.8% and 1.5%.<br/> <br/>When analyzing these website visit campaigns, Tuned Social saw that the campaigns with a CTR of more than 1% had a CPM (cost per million view) that was 2 to 3 times higher than the suggested bidding range for impression-based campaigns.<br/> <br/>The team calculated that, if they switched the campaigns to impression-based bidding while maintaining a CTR of above 1%, they would be buying impressions for much less, and would therefore have a much cheaper CPC.<br/> <br/>They started by bidding at 75% of LinkedIn’s suggested range for best results, which was still much cheaper than the CPM at the time. |
|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|


| What was the result? |
|----------------------|

| By switching to impression-based bidding, and without any changes to the creatives, the campaigns that previously had a CTR of 1% or more now saw:<br/>	•	55% decrease in CPC.<br/>	•	30% increase in conversions (the CPC decrease doesn't directly translate to the conversion increase due to the slight decrease in intent of impression-based bidding).<br/>However, over time, the CTR started to decrease, as LinkedIn optimizes the campaigns towards impressions. This meant that, in some of the cases close to a 1% CTR, a switch back to CPC bidding was necessary. |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

| Why does it work? |
|-------------------|


| With high CTRs, you'll generally get a much cheaper CPC, as well as a greater volume, if you use impression-based objectives. This is because you are only paying for impressions, and not for the high number of clicks you’re receiving. <br/> <br/>In short, the higher the CTR is above 1%, the better this tactic performs.<br/> Want to become an expert in LinkedIn ads?

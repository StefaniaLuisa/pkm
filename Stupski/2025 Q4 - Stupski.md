# **Call Agenda:**

## **1. Frame the Meeting Before Showing Any Screens**

- Clarify expectations for the review: this is a *working session*, not a final design.

- Emphasize the focus on desktop first — mobile is coming, but not a priority for today.

- Set expectations for functionality vs. vision: some animations or interactions may be showcased conceptually due to time constraints, with the option to improve post-launch.

## **2. Establish the Feedback Process**

This is critical — it came up multiple times.

- Ask the group to identify **one point of contact** for all feedback.

- Share preference for consolidated, consensus-based feedback.

- Clarify that if multiple people comment in Figma, *Claire* (or a designated lead) must determine the final direction so we’re not parsing conflicting notes.

- Briefly show them *how* to leave comments in Figma only after the decision-making structure is agreed upon.

## **3. Hero Section Review**

- Notify them that the **play button on the book** has been removed for clarity and to avoid confusing signals.

- Confirm whether the **header/subheader text hierarchy** feels intentional or needs adjustment.

- Clarify whether they consider the “quotes front and center in the hero” as locked.

## **4. Book Assets & Publisher Materials**

- Ask if Wiley will be providing **final book mockups** or photography.

- Confirm whether they expect us to recreate any assets or if their publisher will supply them.

## **5. Video Experience Decisions**

- Clarify:

  - Which video should autoplay in the opening lightbox?

  - Should all videos launch **muted** by default?

  - Should there be more than one video accessible from the hero?

- Confirm whether a **floating video tab** is desired or distracting.

## **6. Main CTA Strategy (“Order Today”)**

This is one of the biggest strategic decisions we need from them.

- Align on what the **primary CTA** should be across the entire experience (likely “Pre-order Today” or “Order Today”).

- Discuss placement — above the fold vs. lower on the page.

- Show the design for the new **vendor-picker pop-up** and explain:

  - Why this creates a smoother user experience.

  - Options: multi-vendor chooser (our recommendation) OR a single preferred vendor.

  - Technical estimate pending from Mike — ask whether they want to explore this enhancement.

## **7. Secondary Button Treatments**

- Review which buttons should appear immediately under the hero and which should appear after scroll, per Claire’s request.

- Confirm styling (ghost outline vs. solid).

## **8. Quote Styling**

- Bring two small decisions for them to finalize:

  - Should quotes use mixed green/white text or a single color?

  - Do they want author names visually emphasized or de-emphasized?

- Note: We’re currently reverting to the original green/white until directed otherwise.

## **9. “What’s Inside” Section**

- Present the updated version without bullets.

- Confirm whether they want:

  - Specific quantified teasers (e.g., “10 revealing examples”)

  - Or more general phrases.

- Clarify that the previous breadcrumb-like style caused confusion, and this version reduces misinterpretation.

## **10. Big Numerical Counter**

- Ask whether **18T** communicates clearly enough, or whether they prefer “18 Trillion” written out.

- Present both animation paths:

  - Counter animation + background color shift (ideal)

  - Static fallback (if dev constraints require it)

- Confirm whether they want the background color shift across the page or limited to the section.

## **11. Meet the Author Section**

- Present the updated Glenn photo.

- Align on whether linking to his **Substack** is acceptable — explain the strategic logic.

- Show the updated CTA styling (button vs. link).

- Ask if they want additional elements (e.g., subtle motif, graphic, or nothing at all).

## **12. Graphic Motifs (“filigree”)**

- Explain the tension between:

  - The organic style of their icons, and

  - The mechanical/cold aesthetic of the book cover.

- Ask whether they want:

  - No motifs at all

  - Very light decorative touches in specific sections

  - A recurring motif across multiple sections for cohesion

- Give them the option to make a decision now or after seeing a few variations.

## **13. Free Chapter Download Block**

- Show both variations (old vs. new).

- Clarify that the updated version is easier to interpret, has better contrast, and aligns more organically with the overall page.

- Confirm they prefer the updated version.

## **14. CTA Block at the Bottom of the Page**

- This is a key decision point — we need their direction.

- Share our recommendation that this CTA may perform better **higher** on the page.

- Explain that the vendor pop-up changes how necessary those mid-page buy buttons are.

- Ask whether they are open to re-placing this section closer to the “About Glenn” segment.

- If not, confirm that they want it as the final element.

## **15. FAQ Section**

- Confirm they are comfortable with the accordion allowing only one question open at a time.

- Ask them to confirm FAQ ordering: alphabetical vs. priority-based.

## **16. Timeline & Post-Launch Enhancements**

- Reinforce that the January launch date is still on track.

- Clarify that some enhancements (animations, interactive motifs, additional polish) may be layered in after launch without delaying the debut.

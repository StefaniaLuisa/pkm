### **Enable Yoast SEO Schema Aggregation (Yoast SEO 27.1+)**

**Effort:** \~30 minutes 

**Prerequisite:** Yoast SEO updated to version 27.1 or later

Yoast just released a new opt-in feature called Schema Aggregation as part of Yoast SEO 27.1 (so we would need to make sure your plugin is udpated to this version to enable). 

What this does is adds a new endpoint to your site that publishes your entire structured data (articles, authors, posts) and organizational information into an format called, "schemamap.” that is optimized for AI search engines to recieve all of your site’s content in one go and see the connected “picture”, rather than adhoc based on what has been scraped/crw one page at a time and not provide sometimes needed context. 

Rather than an AI system crawling every page on your site individually, it can retrieve a clean, connected picture of your content in one go.

This is Yoast's response to the shift toward AI-driven discovery, where people increasingly find information through conversational tools like Microsoft Copilot and other AI agents rather than traditional search.

---

**Our Recommendation: Enable this feature**

We recommend enabling Schema Aggregation for your site. It is a low-risk, forward-looking change that helps ensure your content is accurately represented as AI becomes a bigger part of how people find information online.

Key reasons we recommend it:

- It is completely opt-in and free, available on all Yoast SEO plans.
- It does not change your existing schema or affect how your site appears in traditional search results — the same data is simply organized and served more efficiently.
- It is built with a privacy-first approach and respects your existing Yoast privacy settings.
- It eliminates duplicate entities (e.g., the same author mentioned across many posts) and presents a clean, connected view of your site.
- It uses cached responses and is designed to handle server load efficiently, so it should have no impact on site performance.
- The feature was tested across real-world client sites before launch.

---

**Risks to be aware of**

Before enabling, please consider the following:

**This creates a new public endpoint.** Enabling Schema Aggregation adds a URL to your site (e.g., `/wp-json/yoast/v1/schema-aggregator/get-xml`) that exposes your site's full structured data graph to anyone — including AI systems — who requests it. For most sites, this is simply a more organized version of information already publicly available. However, if your site contains content you intentionally restrict from broad indexing or AI use, you should evaluate whether this aligns with your content strategy before enabling it.

**It is an early-stage standard.** NLWeb is a proposed new standard, not yet a finalized or universally adopted one. The ecosystem of AI agents actively consuming schemamaps is still developing. The practical benefit may be limited today, but is expected to grow over time.

**It is a new feature.** It launched in March 2026. While Yoast tested it in controlled environments with agency partners, it has not yet had a long track record in the wild. We do not anticipate issues, but as with any new feature, it is worth monitoring your site after enabling it.

---

**How to enable it**

1. Ensure Yoast SEO is updated to version 27.1 or later.
2. Open the Yoast SEO settings in your WordPress dashboard. A guided walkthrough will appear introducing the feature.
3. Use the toggle to enable Schema Aggregation (also referred to as the NLWeb integration in your settings).
4. No further configuration is required.

For more detail, refer to the [Yoast help article on enabling Schema Aggregation](https://yoast.com/yoast-seo-march-3-2026/).

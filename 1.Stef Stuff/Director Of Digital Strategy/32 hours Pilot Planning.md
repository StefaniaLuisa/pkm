### AI Summary Purpose of the Meeting

This meeting advanced the planning for a **32-hour workweek pilot** centered on shifting from **hours-based thinking to value-based delivery**, without abandoning time tracking. The session focused on feasibility, workload realism, pilot structure, and the operational systems required to support this shift at scale.

The conversation was exploratory but decisive. The pilot is not launching January 1—it is entering a **planning and validation phase** in January, with Stef’s portfolio audit as the critical first input.

---

### Key Agreements & Direction

**1. The 32-hour pilot is a planning transition, not an immediate switch**
Bryan confirmed that Q1 will be used as a transition period. Given new retainers, onboarding demands, and leadership bandwidth, a hard shift before late February or March is unrealistic. The goal is steady progress toward a sustainable model, not a symbolic cutoff.

**2. Time tracking stays—pressure around hours does not**
Stef will continue logging time in Productive for forecasting and planning. What changes is the *psychological weight* of retainer hours. The pilot aims to decouple perceived value from hour-by-hour accounting, not eliminate tracking.

**3. The pilot does not require client-facing disclosure**
There is no requirement to tell clients they are part of a pilot. The intent is to keep the external experience consistent while adjusting internal planning, prioritization, and value articulation behind the scenes.

**4. The pilot must be viable across all clients, not just one**
Although one client will be used as a test case, the model must work at the portfolio level. This means auditing *all*current clients to understand true workload, not optimizing for a single “ideal” scenario.

### Stef’s Portfolio Audit (Core Input to the Pilot)

The primary deliverable coming out of this meeting is a **written portfolio audit** from Stef that answers one core question:

> *Is a 32-hour week feasible given current clients, real work performed, and the level of strategic value expected?*

The audit should include:

- A client-by-client assessment of actual time spent, including:

  - Planning and prep

  - Cross-team coordination

  - Strategic thinking that is currently under-tracked

- An honest evaluation of:

  - Where time is required vs. optional

  - Where work expands due to interest vs. necessity

- A realistic view of January as a planning-heavy month

- A clear delta between logged time and actual effort

- Identification of one client that may be appropriate for piloting the value-forward model

The output should be **a simple written document**, not a spreadsheet, Gantt chart, or Productive overhaul. Bryan explicitly requested baby steps to avoid version sprawl and rework.

---

### Strategic Shift in How Value Is Delivered

A major throughline of the conversation was reframing Stef’s role—and eventually the agency’s role—as **proactive strategic leadership**, not reactive execution.

Key expectations moving forward:

- Strategists proactively bring:

  - Long-term testing plans

  - Optimization and analytics roadmaps

  - Quarterly or phased initiatives tied to outcomes

- Value is demonstrated through:

  - Clear milestones

  - Learning narratives (“what we tried, what we learned, what’s next”)

  - Strategic recommendations, not just task completion

- Retainers should feel like:

  - Ongoing strategic partnership

  - Not a queue for incoming requests

This aligns strongly with work Stef is *already doing* (strategic questionnaires, planning conversations, cross-functional alignment) but has not historically been formalized or credited as value.

---

### Productive, AI, and “Value-Driven Context”

A significant portion of the meeting explored how to operationalize value-based reporting using Productive and AI.

Key conclusions:

- **Productive remains the source of truth**

- Value context should live as close to the work as possible—initially at the **task level**

- Internal comments (not public client comments) will be used to capture:

  - Strategic rationale

  - Client reactions or quotes

  - Measurable outcomes or insights

- Bryan will draft an SOP for this “context stuffing” approach, with the intent to:

  - Enable richer AI-generated reporting

  - Reduce scattered knowledge across Slack, Docs, and memory

  - Support future marketing, case studies, and upsell narratives

Milestones may later evolve into outcome-level reporting structures, but the group agreed not to overload Productive prematurely or misuse features without clarity.

### Meetings, Planning Time, and Guardrails

There was strong alignment on the following principles:

- Meetings must remain intentional, agenda-driven, and purposeful

- Knowledge sharing is valuable but should be:

  - Asynchronous where possible

  - Monthly rather than weekly

- Planning time is legitimate work and must be accounted for

- Stef’s tendency to overextend is acknowledged and will be actively managed

- The pilot is explicitly *not* permission to work more—it is permission to work **smarter and with clarity**

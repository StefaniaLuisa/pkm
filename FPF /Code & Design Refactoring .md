As FPF continues to roll out new sections/pages of your website with blocks that are either new or have enhanced features, I'd like to recommend we take steps towards "refactoring" your website - which essentially means bringing existing blocks up to the same technical functionality and design standards as any new ones that have been created recently ( Center for AI, Events, PEN Member).

Refactoring allows us to future-proof your site, ensuring that all of your site's elements and common tech functionality is shared, creating new pages and sections become much faster on the design and dev side, as well as for your team internally - it all will take less time, which will be less expensive in the long-run.

Here are the areas that are involved in a refactor.

I am recommending this task be started in the upcoming months, because of the bigger block design and functionality updates that have been taking place with the most recent redesigns and creations of the Center for AI, Events Landing Page, etc.

Furthermore, refactoring blocks is the fastest way to ensure your entire site meets current accessibility standards (see: WCAG). This is not just about legal compliance—it’s about making sure every visitor, regardless of ability, can fully engage with your mission and resources.

Mobile Responsiveness Is Essential
With over half of all web traffic coming from mobile devices (Statista, 2024), seamless mobile experiences aren’t optional. Updating old blocks ensures every page—from key action forms to impact stories—works beautifully for mobile users, directly supporting campaign conversion and engagement.

Outdated code and legacy blocks are more likely to introduce bugs, security vulnerabilities, and higher maintenance costs (Atlassian). Refactoring now avoids costly, piecemeal fixes later and frees up your team to focus on mission-critical projects.

Future-Proof for Growth and Change
When all site elements share a common technical foundation, adding new campaigns or features becomes faster and less expensive. This sets FPF up for future growth without “rebuilding the wheel” every time priorities evolve.

Faster Performance and Stronger SEO
Modernized, efficient code improves site speed and search engine rankings (Google Search Central). This means more people find, visit, and engage with your content—critical for both impact and funding.

Enable Reliable Analytics and Integrations

**Why a Sitewide Audit and Refactor of Blocks is Critical for FPF’s Success**

As we introduce new, modern blocks and features to your site, it’s vital to ensure all existing blocks are also updated (refactored) to the same technical and design standards. Here’s why this isn’t just a “nice to have”—it’s essential for your foundation’s digital health, growth, and impact:

**1. Consistency Drives Trust and Credibility**

- Inconsistent layouts, design, or mobile responsiveness across your pages can erode trust, especially for a nonprofit foundation advocating for privacy and data responsibility. A unified experience signals professionalism and reliability, which encourages supporters, partners, and donors to engage more deeply.

**2. Improved Accessibility and Inclusion**

- As you refactor blocks, you ensure all your site sections meet up-to-date accessibility standards. This isn’t just about compliance—it’s about serving everyone, including users with disabilities, ensuring your mission and resources reach the widest possible audience.

**3. Mobile Responsiveness Is Non-Negotiable**

- More than 50% of global web traffic is mobile. Refactoring old blocks ensures every page and action (like donating or signing up) works beautifully on all devices. Otherwise, you risk losing visitors—and donations—from anyone on a phone or tablet.

**4. Lower Technical Debt = Lower Costs Long-Term**

- Outdated blocks can cause bugs, increase maintenance costs, and even lead to security risks. Fixing problems before they compound means your team isn’t paying more down the line for urgent, piecemeal fixes. This proactive approach saves money and keeps your budget focused on mission-critical work.

**5. Future-Proofing for Growth**

- Refactored blocks make it far easier (and cheaper) to add new features, campaigns, or sections in the future. You’re not just updating for today, but setting up your website to scale with your evolving needs—without having to “start over” each time.

**6. Enhanced Performance and SEO**

- Clean, up-to-date code leads to faster load times and better search rankings, which means more people can find and engage with your content. Every second counts, especially for visitors with slower connections or those using assistive technology.

**7. Better Analytics and Integrations**

- Modernized blocks enable accurate tracking of donations, actions, and engagement—giving you the data you need to report on impact, apply for grants, and refine your strategy.

---

**For nonprofits, every dollar and every impression counts. Investing in a comprehensive code and design audit now ensures your site continues to serve your mission—seamlessly, accessibly, and credibly—for years to come.**

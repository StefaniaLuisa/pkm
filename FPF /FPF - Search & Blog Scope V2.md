
### Scope of Work: Blog Search and Content Taxonomy Enhancement

**Client:** Future of Privacy Forum (FPF)
**Project:** Blog Search and Categorization Optimization
**Website:** <https://fpf.org/blog/>

---

### Project Overview

This initiative focuses on upgrading the Future of Privacy Forum's (FPF) blog infrastructure to improve content discoverability, search utility, and backend editorial efficiency. The project will leverage modern WordPress capabilities and targeted SEO improvements to restructure the blog’s taxonomy, streamline filtering and categorization, and increase content accessibility and visibility. These enhancements will support a better user experience for FPF’s key audiences—policy professionals, academics, stakeholders in the privacy community, and the general public.

---

### Objectives

- Enhance content discovery with expanded search filters and improved keyword relevance.

- Refactor existing taxonomy structures to create a more intuitive, scalable organizational framework.

- Ensure legacy content is aligned with current SEO best practices to maximize reach and search visibility.

- Improve backend usability for editorial and communications teams managing the blog.

---

### Project Deliverables

#### 1. Advanced Search Enhancements

Utilizing WordPress-native tools and premium plugins (e.g., SearchWP and FacetWP), we will:

- Build out faceted search capabilities allowing users to filter content by:

  - Content Type (e.g., Blog, Report, Event, Case Study, Press Release)

  - Subject Area (e.g., Artificial Intelligence, Privacy Law, Youth and Education, Data Ethics)

  - Publication Date (rolling and custom-range filters)

  - Author (leveraging Co-Authors Plus to better manage contributor data)

#### 2. Taxonomy Audit and Redesign

- Conduct a comprehensive review of existing categories, subcategories, and tags to identify redundancies and underutilized structures.

- Propose a reorganized category tree that aligns with FPF’s thematic pillars and content strategy.

- Establish updated editorial guidelines for consistent tagging and categorization of new and existing content.

#### 3. SEO Refactoring

- Perform a content-level SEO audit of the blog archive to identify high-value and underperforming assets.

- Refine metadata, headers, alt text, and internal linking to improve page authority and search rankings.

- Add [schema.org](http://schema.org) structured data to qualifying content types for improved indexing and display in search engine results.

#### 4. Technical Deployment

- Configure SearchWP for custom weighting and search indexing.

- Integrate FacetWP to provide front-end filtering elements (checkboxes, dropdowns, etc.) with minimal load time.

- Update Co-Authors Plus plugin settings and author profiles to accurately reflect contributor data.

- Conduct responsive design QA and accessibility testing to ensure full mobile and WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.

---

### Key Outcomes

- An enhanced search and filtering interface integrated directly into the blog experience.

- A refined taxonomy that simplifies navigation and improves editorial workflows.

- SEO-optimized legacy content, increasing site traffic and content lifespan.

- A post-launch content management guide including a walkthrough video and taxonomy usage reference.

### Stakeholder Requirements

To ensure timely progress, the following inputs are required from FPF:

- Administrator-level credentials for WordPress, plugin dashboard, and theme editor.

- Approval on revised taxonomy structure and visual filter UI.

- A finalized list of active contributors for author filtering and profile updates.

- Identification of high-priority blog posts to be included in SEO optimization scope.

---

### Exclusions

This scope does not include:

- Creation of new blog content or editorial writing.

- A visual or structural redesign of the full blog layout beyond filter components.

- Post-launch technical support or maintenance, unless included in a separate agreement.

# Summary of meeting

## **1. Timeline Clarification**

Melissa’s first question was around overall project timing. She expected a loose timeline in the SOW and wanted to understand whether this is a 2-month effort, 3 months, or closer to 6.

I shared the following:

- Based on the complexity and the interconnected parts of the Reading Room, it’s **not a 6-month project**.

- A realistic estimate is **2–3 months** from kickoff through launch.

- Actual scheduling depends on **December and January resourcing**, which is why we need Heather’s review to confirm what’s feasible.

She mainly wanted reassurance that this isn’t a half-year build, and knowing we’re in the 2–3 month range helps her plan for leadership expectations.

### **Sally’s availability note:**

- Last in-person touchpoint with Sally in 2024: **December 15**

- Next in-person opportunity after that: **January 12**
  Melissa noted that design approvals and major decision-making are easiest when Sally is in the room, so we may need to structure early design work around these dates.

---

## **2. Budget Questions & Retainer Hours**

Her second question was whether all 150 estimated hours need to be entirely new project hours, or whether some of this could come from their retainer.

I explained:

- We can explore a **hybrid model**, but we need to avoid cannibalizing their monthly support work.

- I’ll review how many hours they typically use month-to-month and identify how many hours (if any) could consistently be allocated to the Reading Room without impacting their ongoing needs.

She said even **a small recurring allocation** (e.g., \~5 hours/month) would help bring the total project cost down and make the budget conversation with leadership easier. There is very little hesitation from Brian or Melissa if Sally wants this project—which she does—but Melissa is still aiming to be responsible with budget planning.

---

## **3. Possible Scope Reduction: Book Club Hub**

If they need to scale back the phase-one scope, Melissa suggested that the **Book Club Hub** is the most natural piece to phase out for now.

Reason:

- Gambrell is launching a *partner-led book club series* with Ally Foundation and Charlotte Mecklenburg Library starting Q1 2025.

- All event logistics and RSVPs will live on the library’s website, which has significantly more traffic.

- Because of that, the internal Book Club Hub for the Reading Room is not required for immediate launch.

She wants us to model:

- Full scope with Book Club Hub

- Reduced scope without it

- Impacts on hours, cost, and timeline

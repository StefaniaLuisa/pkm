## **Project Description**

The Gambrell Foundation is piloting an interactive digital “Reading Room,” called the Wall of Books, to showcase how literature shapes the foundation’s worldview, grantmaking, and programming. The pilot will launch with roughly 20–25 books, including a core set of 8–12 “Essential” titles that anchor Gambrell’s thinking, plus the first two thematic clusters (for example, “Awe & Wonder” and “Purpose & Meaning”). The experience is intended to feel exploratory and visual, inviting visitors to discover how books connect to each other and to bigger ideas.

The centerpiece of the Reading Room will be a constellation-style view that displays books as interconnected nodes. Users can pan and zoom through this constellation, hover over books to see titles, and visually see clusters by theme. The interface will group books into clusters like “Awe & Wonder” or “Purpose & Meaning” so users can see how ideas sit in relation to each other. For visitors who prefer a more traditional interface, we will also provide a grid or list view so they can browse all titles at a glance.

Clicking on any book will open a Book Detail overlay that surfaces:

- Cover image, title, and author

- A short synopsis

- A concise Gambrell commentary (2–4 lines on why the book matters)

- Thematic tags and categories

- Links to related Gambrell programs, content, or media

- “Similar to…” recommendations that encourage deeper exploration

These details will be interconnected so users can move from book to book, navigate by theme, or jump into related Gambrell initiatives without losing their place.

The Reading Room will be built on a scalable and flexible content structure. While the initial phase we will launch with roughly 25 titles, the system will be created to support up to 200 books over time without affecting performance or usability. A well-planned taxonomy will help organize content by theme, topic, and related Gambrell initiatives so visitors will always have a clear path to discover more as the library grows.

The pilot will also introduce a small set of interactive features to extend the Reading Room beyond a static library:

- **“Good Life” Bingo (Prototype)**
  A conceptual prototype for a recurring digital game tied to Reading Room themes. Approximately six times a year, Gambrell may run a “Good Life Bingo” challenge, accessible via QR codes at events or in printed materials. The pilot will include a 5x5 bingo grid of action prompts inspired by book themes (for example, “Go on an awe walk in nature”). Users will conceptually “check off” experiences as they complete them. For this phase, the focus is on the concept, layout, and visual treatment, with the understanding that fuller interactivity can be built later.

- **Book Recommendation Quiz**
  A short, interactive quiz that asks visitors a few questions about their interests, mood, or what they are seeking. Based on their answers, the quiz will recommend one or more books from the Wall of Books collection. The goal is to help new visitors find an easy starting point and to personalize the experience without adding technical complexity.

- **Book Club Hub**
  A dedicated space to support Gambrell’s emerging book club activities. This will include a template for recap posts (summaries and reflections from past book club meetings) and an RSVP pattern for upcoming events. Visitors will be able to see what the book club is reading now, browse past selections, and sign up for future discussions.

Behind the scenes, the Wall of Books will be built on a scalable content structure. While the pilot will focus on about 25 titles, the system will be designed to comfortably support up to approximately 200 books over time without sacrificing performance or usability. A robust taxonomy will organize content by theme, topic, and related Gambrell initiatives so visitors can continue to filter and discover content even as the library grows.

The Reading Room will be fully integrated into [gambrellfoundation.org](http://gambrellfoundation.org) and aligned with Gambrell’s existing brand standards. The experience should feel like a natural extension of the site, using existing typography, color palette, and tone, while introducing new interactive patterns. The primary experience will be optimized for desktop to support the constellation-style exploration, but we will also provide a simplified, mobile-friendly path that prioritizes the grid/list views and comfortable reading on smaller screens.

Overall, this pilot blends storytelling, technology, and design to create an engaging digital space for bibliographic exploration. It will help visitors see the constellation of ideas that inform “a great life” through the books Gambrell values and will lay the groundwork for future phases of the Reading Room.

The target for the pilot launch is early 2026, ideally February 2026. A proposed high-level timeline:

- **November – December 2025: Kickoff and Design**

  - Project kickoff and discovery

  - Collection of book list, commentary, themes, and related content

  - Iterative UX/UI design and prototyping for constellation, grid/list, quiz, bingo, and Book Club hub

  - Target: approve core UX/UI by end of December

- **Early January 2026: Development Begins**

  - Build front-end and back-end components

  - Set up content types, fields, and taxonomies

  - Develop constellation, Book Detail overlays, quiz, bingo prototype, and Book Club hub

  - Begin content population with pilot titles

- **Late January 2026: Testing and Refinement**

  - Internal QA on staging

  - Client review and one round of consolidated feedback

  - Incorporate design and content tweaks

  - Finalize training materials

- **Early February 2026: Training and Launch**

  - Conduct staff training session

  - Final pre-launch checks and deployment

  - Target public launch on or before mid-February 2026

- **February – March 2026: Post-Launch Support**

  - Monitor site performance and address any issues

  - Make minor refinements based on early usage

  - Discuss next-phase enhancements as appropriate

This timeline assumes that core content (book list, commentary, quiz questions, and bingo prompts) is available by the design phase and that feedback cycles are timely. If content is delayed or new features are added, we will adjust the schedule and communicate risks to the target launch date.

## **Assumptions and Dependencies**

This scope is based on the following assumptions. If any of these change, scope, budget, or timeline may need to be revisited.

**Content Provision**

- Gambrell will provide:

  - Book list with titles and authors

  - Cover images

  - Synopses or summaries

  - Gambrell commentary blurbs

  - Quiz questions and mappings to recommended books

  - Initial Good Life Bingo prompts

Timely delivery of content is critical to stay on schedule.

**Existing Platform and Integration**

- The Wall of Books will live within Gambrell’s existing website and hosting environment.

- We will use the current CMS (assumed WordPress) and existing navigation, header, and footer patterns.

- No new complex third-party systems or custom user account features are included (for example, we are not building a separate login system, user-profiles, or a full game engine).

If we discover that a separate subsite or special hosting is required for the visualization, we will evaluate options and may propose a scope adjustment.

**Design Assets and Branding**

- Gambrell will provide brand assets (logos, style guides, imagery) at project start.

- Any new icons or illustrations created by 4Site will align with the existing brand and be approved during design reviews.

**Feedback and Decision-Making**

- Gambrell will designate a single primary point of contact who will:

  - Consolidate internal feedback

  - Provide clear approvals and direction

- Scope includes up to two rounds of review for each major deliverable (design and staging site). Additional rounds or large changes in direction may require a change order.

**Scope Boundaries**

- This SOW covers the Reading Room and directly related features (constellation, book details, quiz, bingo prototype, Book Club hub).

- It does not include unrelated website sections or advanced functionality such as:

  - A fully featured social network around the books

  - Large-scale personalization algorithms

  - Building out the full 200-book library in this initial phase

Future phases can extend the pilot to include richer functionality once the core experience is live and tested.

**Hosting and Security**

- Gambrell’s existing hosting environment is assumed to be sufficient for anticipated traffic and the added visualization.

- Gambrell or its IT provider will continue to manage core hosting, security monitoring, and backups.

- 4Site will follow best practices and avoid introducing known-vulnerable libraries.

**Launch and Promotion**

- Gambrell will lead marketing and promotion for the launch (such as email, social posts, and event collateral featuring QR codes).

- 4Site’s role is to ensure the Reading Room is technically ready and aligned with any promotional plans.

## **Budget Estimate**

Below is our estimated level of effort to design, build, and launch the Wall of Books pilot. The budget is expressed in hours by service category and can be translated to a fixed project fee using our standard rate.

- **Strategy and Planning – 5 hours**
  Discovery and kickoff prep, alignment on goals, and ongoing strategic oversight.

- **Design and Prototyping – 50 hours**
  UX and UI design, interactive prototypes for the Reading Room, constellation, book details, quiz, bingo prototype, and responsive layouts. Includes up to two feedback cycles.

- **Development – 70 hours**
  Front-end and back-end development, including:

  - Constellation visualization and interactivity

  - WordPress configurations (custom post types, fields, taxonomies)

  - Quiz implementation

  - Bingo prototype layout

  - Book Club hub templates

  - Integration and internal testing during build

- **Content Integration – 5 hours**
  Initial setup of 20–25 books, formatting of provided content, and configuration of the first quiz flow and bingo prompts.

- **Quality Assurance and Launch – 10 hours**
  Browser and device testing, bug fixes, and launch coordination.

- **Training and Documentation – 5 hours**
  Staff training session and written admin documentation.

- **Project Management – 15 hours**
  Ongoing coordination, status check-ins, scheduling, and communication between 4Site and Gambrell.

- **Post-Launch Support – 10 hours**
  Two-week stabilization window to address issues, minor adjustments, and questions.

**Total:** 170 hours
At our standard rate of **$210/hour**, the estimated project fee is **$35,700** (170 hours × $210/hour). We do not anticipate additional expenses such as travel or software costs for this engagement.

This estimate reflects our current understanding of the scope. If Gambrell chooses to expand functionality or significantly increase content during the project, we will revisit the estimate and discuss any necessary budget adjustments in advance. Our goal is to deliver a strong, future-ready pilot within this budget by focusing on the agreed priorities and maintaining clear communication throughout the project.
